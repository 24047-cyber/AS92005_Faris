Methods of water quality management

1. Screening: Filters large debris through metal screens. 2. Coagulation: Clumps contaminants using aluminium sulfate. 3. Clarification (Sedimentation): Settles abd removes heavy particles. 4. Filtration: Traps remaining particles using sand. 5. Disinfection: Kills germs by adding chlorine. 6. Fluoridation: Adds fluoride to the water supply. 7. pH Balancing: Normalises pH in the final stage.

Water supplies in New Zealand are typically sourced through surface water (rivers, streams, lakes), groundwater, and rainwater.

New Zealand doesn't primarily rely on (or even have) desalination plants and seawater, as it is more complicated and costly.

Purification plants have regular monitoring, to locate and resolve any issues if necessary.
